Writing prompts for video scripts.
intent
A short voiceover script for a cinematic video about early morning in Tokyo — quiet, contemplative, no dialogue.
prompt
Write a 30-second voiceover script for a cinematic video. Scene: early morning in Tokyo, 6am. Empty streets, distant train sounds, a convenience store worker arranging shelves. Tone: calm, contemplative. Style: NHK documentary. No dialogue, only narration. End with a single poetic line.
output
The city exhales before it wakes. At six in the morning, Tokyo belongs only to those who know its silence — the delivery driver turning a corner, the vending machine humming its patient note. The convenience store on Shinjuku-dori opens its doors to no one in particular, and the light inside falls onto the pavement like a quiet announcement. This is the city before it becomes itself.

intent
Scene description for a short film — a woman waiting at a train station at night, feeling ambivalent about leaving.
prompt
Write a 60-word scene description for a short film. Character: a woman in her 30s, standing alone on a platform at 11pm. She has a suitcase. The last train is approaching. She hasn't decided whether to board. No internal monologue. Describe only what the camera sees. Tone: melancholic but not sentimental. Tense: present.
output
Platform 3. A woman stands at the yellow line, one hand on the handle of a hard-shell suitcase. The board above her clicks to the final departure. She watches the train approach — headlights sweeping the concrete, the warm rush of displaced air lifting her hair. Her hand does not move. The doors open. She looks at the gap between the train and the platform edge.

Strong window light with visible dust particles. Leather chairs skewed more modern than intended. Adding "vintage" on retry produced better aged texture. The espresso cup rendered too large — "espresso macchiato" gave better scale. Third attempt nailed the grain and shadow quality.
